# Writing Guidance

## General

- Use English unless the user explicitly asks for another language.
- Keep each asset's title, description, and keywords in one language.
- Keep descriptions concrete, factual, and visually grounded.
- Optimize for stock search intent without adding unsupported claims.

## Titles

- Lead with the strongest visible subject.
- Keep the title short, factual, and readable.
- Prefer noun-driven titles such as `Alpaca Portrait` or `Fern Fiddlehead`.
- Avoid filler phrases like `in nature` or `outdoors` unless they add real search value.

## Descriptions

- Mention the main subject early.
- Prefer one factual sentence.
- Include commercially useful visual facts such as `close up`, `portrait`, `perched`, `resting`, `tropical foliage`, or `glasshouse` only when clearly visible.
- Avoid diary-style captions, hype, and subjective language.

## Keywords

- Put the strongest 5 to 10 searchable terms first.
- Order keywords roughly as subject, action, setting, distinctive detail, location.
- Remove duplicates and weak filler terms.
- Add synonyms only when they improve search value and stay visibly true.
- Do not add unsupported concepts, brands, private information, camera metadata, or species/location claims.

## Subject Priority

- If a clear human subject is visible, lead with person, people, portrait, action, or role terms before background scenery.
- For animals, plants, textures, and architecture, lead with the main visible subject before broader setting terms.
- If the frame is weak or cluttered for stock use, note the caveat in `Notes` instead of forcing overconfident metadata.
